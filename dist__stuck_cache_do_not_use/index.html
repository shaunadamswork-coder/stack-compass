Understanding the 12-month commission cap

Several of these programs pay recurring commission only for a capped period per referral, and the caps measure different things — it helps to read the rate and the window together rather than comparing percentages alone:

  • GetResponse pays its tiered 40–60% rate for up to 12 months per referral (verified 2026-07-22). After that window, payout on that specific referral ends.
  • Kit pays 50% recurring for the first 12 months per referral, and affiliates who reach high-volume status tiers can continue earning 10%, 15%, or 20% recurring beyond that window (verified 2026-07-22).
  • Systeme.io works differently: it isn't a payout cap, it's a 1-year attribution window on the referral itself (verified 2026-07-22) — a sale has to happen within a year of the referral click for the commission to count at all. That's a different mechanic than a "recurring payout duration," and it should not be described as "lifetime" commissions.
  • GoHighLevel's 40% + 5% structure doesn't publish a stated recurring-payout cap in the same way, but a commission only qualifies after 45 days of active, good-standing referral status (verified 2026-07-22).

Because these mechanics differ (payout duration vs. click attribution vs. qualification period), a flat percentage comparison can be misleading on its own.

FAQ

Which of these programs pays the highest commission rate?

Systeme.io advertises the highest flat rate at 60% recurring (verified 2026-07-22). GetResponse can also reach 60% recurring, but only at its top volume tier, and that payout is capped at 12 months per referral (verified 2026-07-22) — a structural difference from Systeme.io's flat rate.

Does "60% recurring" mean the same thing for Systeme.io and GetResponse?

No. Systeme.io's 60% is a flat recurring rate paired with a 1-year attribution window (verified 2026-07-22). GetResponse's 60% is the top of a three-step tier (40% → 50% → 60%) reserved for higher-volume affiliates and is capped at 12 months per individual referral (verified 2026-07-22).

Is GoHighLevel a good fit for a solo creator?

It can be, but GoHighLevel's core design centers on white-label, multi-client management, which is more relevant to agencies. Its commission also requires 45 days of active good-standing before a referral qualifies for payout (verified 2026-07-22), a longer qualification runway than the other three programs.

What network do these affiliate programs run through?

GetResponse and Kit both run through PartnerStack (verified 2026-07-22). Systeme.io and GoHighLevel are vendor-direct programs.

Are Teachable, Kajabi, Brevo, and Semrush part of the same comparison?

They're adjacent tools solving different problems (course hosting, transactional email, SEO) rather than direct competitors to the four core stack tools above. They use different payout models entirely — Teachable pays 30% recurring in year one on a 30-day cookie, Kajabi is tiered up to a 100% first-month bounty, Brevo runs a bounty program, and Semrush pays a flat $200 per sale.

Do any of these programs guarantee affiliate earnings?

No. Commission rate, cookie window, and payout cap are contractual terms of each affiliate program, not projections or guarantees of income. Actual earnings depend on referral volume, customer retention, and program terms that can change.
